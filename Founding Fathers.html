The Story

In 1968, Great Britain announced its decision to terminate, by 1 December 1971, the treaty relationship and with it the British protection of the seven sheikhdoms, as well as Bahrain and Qatar. The nine parties attempted to form a union, but by mid-1971 they were unable to agree on terms. Both Bahrain and Qatar decided to become separate, independent states, in August and September 1971. On 2 December 1971, six of the seven sheikhdoms entered into a union called the United Arab Emirates. These were Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Fujaira, Sharja, Ajman, and Umm al-Quwain. The seventh, Ras al-Khaima, joined the union in early 1972.
